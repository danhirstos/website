About Me

Hey, I'm Dan, a Data Scientist currently working for Ordnance Survey. I love escape rooms, meditation, and baking. Early in university, I discovered a passion for uniting remote sensing and big data. I love working on products that use satellite imagery to gain a new perspective into the world around us. Career

Ordnance Survey

Data Scientist June 2020 - Present

I've been working at Ordnance Survey since August 2021 as part of their Rapid Prototyping Team. Our team develops new ideas quickly - between 2 and 6 weeks per project. Such rapid iteration has taught me to develop minimum viable products quickly and collaboratively using a large variety of frameworks and techniques. One example project is the development of the osdatahub Python library for Ordnance Survey's APIs

Earth-i

Developer June 2020 - July 2021

Between 2020 and 2021, I worked as a developer at Earth-i Ltd. Here, I learned to devise scalable applications using cloud computing, built models using machine learning, and used different sensors to gain insight into a variety of industries. I particularly focused on the SAVANT project, taking on a role as a principal technical architect of the data pipeline.

Chapel Thrill Escapes

Co-Founder and Treasurer October 2018 - March 2020

Some friends and I established Chapel Thrill Escapes, the first student-built, student-led, and student funded escape room. It is a non-profit dedicated to providing opportunities for creative synergy and sustainable employment for students at UNC Chapel Hill. I helped incorporate the charity, acquire start-up capital, and build puzzles to create our first room - the Belltower. You can see the room here.

SEDS USA

Chair November 2018 - November 2019

I was incredibly lucky to be elected as Chair for SEDS USA, the world's largest student-run space organisation. I worked with a wonderful team to support 85 chapters across the US, provide funding and scholarship opportunities, and host a conference with over 500 attendees.

Satellite Applications Catapult

Software Engineering Intern Summer 2019

Whilst working at the catapult, I worked on a classifier to detect artisanal small-scale mining across the developing world. I built a pipeline that generated datasets from large Sentinel-2 tiles and used it to create a binary classification model. I later adapted the dataset generator to also support Sentinel-1 imagery. Although I followed a naive approach in creating the classifier, I learned valuable lessons about manipulating open-source satellite imagery and machine learning.
